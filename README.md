# computer-vision

📈 PhysioNet ECG Image Digitization Solution🚀
OverviewIn this project: I developed a sophisticated multi-stage computer vision pipeline to tackle the challenge of ECG Digitization. My solution converts physical ECG paper records (scans or photos) into high-fidelity digital signals. This is achieved by combining deep learning for segmentation with geometric transformations for rectification.
🏆 Competition Results & Performance: My methodology achieved a high ranking on the Kaggle Leaderboard by focusing on transparency and rigorous Visual Quality Assurance (QA).Public Score: 18.17 (SNR dB)Rank: Top 15

🛠 My Methodology & Pipeline Architecture: I designed the solution as a modular Pipeline Class, allowing for independent debugging and optimization of each stage.Stage 0: Geometric NormalizationTo handle images taken at different angles or rotations:Model: ResNet-based Stage0Net.Action: I implemented 13-point landmark detection to identify lead markers and determine the correct image orientation.Result: All input images are automatically rotated and standardized to a fixed 3024×4032 resolution.Stage 1: Grid Rectification (Perspective Correction)Real-world paper is often wrinkled or photographed with perspective distortion:Model: ResNet34-Unet (Stage1Net).Action: The model detects the underlying grid coordinates. I then applied Homography transformations to "flatten" the paper.Result: A perfectly aligned grid where time and voltage axes are linear and predictable.Stage 2: Segmentation & Waveform ExtractionThe core of my digitization engine:Model: U-Net with CoordConv layers (Net3).Architecture: By using CoordConv, I allowed the model to maintain spatial awareness of where it is in the ECG strip.Process: After generating a segmentation mask, I developed a pixel_to_series conversion logic to extract numerical values. I also applied Einthoven’s Law ($Lead II = Lead I + Lead III$) to ensure mathematical consistency between leads.
📂 Project Structure & Innovationsphysionet-ecg-images-digitization.ipynb: My full end-to-end implementation code.Visual QA System: I built a custom visualization framework to inspect the output of every stage (Scan → Rotated → Rectified → Mask → Signal).Robust Pre-processing: My pipeline handles noise, shadows, and varying paper textures through advanced image normalization techniques.⚙️ How to UseOpen the .ipynb notebook.The Pipeline Class I created allows you to process any image by simply calling pipeline.process(image_path).The results are automatically formatted into the submission.csv format required for the PhysioNet challenge.
